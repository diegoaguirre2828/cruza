# Cruzar — FB group posts (Nati's alt account)

**Voice:** first-person, casual RGV Spanish, no emojis, no marketing words ("app," "founder," "we," "platform"). Nati is a regular cruzante who found something useful and shares it.

**Why no emojis:** per `feedback_cruzar_fb_reply_voice.md`. Border-crossing groups are mostly text-only adults; emojis read as marketing.

**Why first-person:** Nati is a person, not a brand account. Self-disclosure creates trust. A post that says "yo uso" converts way better than "this app does."

**Posting cadence:** rotate through these — never the SAME variant twice in one group. Spread across days. **Comment on 2-3 other people's posts** in the group BEFORE posting your own — establishes presence first.

**Link form:** use slug URLs (`cruzar.app/cruzar/los-tomates`) for specific bridges or `cruzar.app/camaras` for the cameras hub. Both are pasteable and human-readable.

## Psychology principles loaded into these (per Sidera DNA)

1. **Reciprocity** — give helpful info first, link is the byproduct (V1, V2)
2. **Self-disclosure** — Nati shares HER experience, vulnerable framing creates trust (V8, V11)
3. **Social proof through specificity** — concrete bridge + concrete wait time = "this is real" (every variant uses real numbers)
4. **In-group identity** — "los del valle", "los que cruzan diario" = belonging cue (V5, V13)
5. **Loss aversion** — "no agarrar el peor momento" = avoid pain framing (V11)
6. **Curiosity** — "le voló la cabeza" = something worth checking (V6, V7)
7. **Anti-marketing** — first-person, lowercase, missing punctuation, casual = NOT a brand (every variant)

---

## Hard rules (do not violate)

1. **One link per post max.** Two links = spam flag.
2. **No "cruzar.app" without a path** — always /camaras or /cruzar/<slug> or /datos. Bare domain reads as marketing.
3. **No "instala la app"** anywhere. Goal is browser visit, install happens organically via the install sheet on /camaras.
4. **No same exact post twice in same group.** FB shadowbans repeats.
5. **Don't reply to your own post.** Looks fake.
6. **Don't tag the Cruzar Page** — keeps Nati's account separate from the official page (per `project_cruzar_no_nati_no_page_automation_20260417.md`).
7. **If a group admin asks about the link** → admit "es una página que me gusta, no es mía." Don't claim ownership.

## V2 conversion notes (Sidera DNA loaded)

What makes V2 different from V1:
- **Promise alignment**: post mentions "te avisa cuando baje" → user lands on /camaras → sticky bar at 30s says exactly that → tap → signup. Click expectation matches landing experience = lower bounce
- **Action verb in present tense**: "me avisa", "ya no salgo sin", "me dice" — Nati is currently using it, not recommending it as a future thing. Higher trust
- **Specific outcome**: "ya no agarro el puente lleno", "ya no me llama mi mama", "me ahorré hora y media" — concrete results > vague benefits
- **Light emotional anchor**: "cambio de vida", "cambio total", "ya no jodo a nadie" — small frame of relief, not big sales pitch

## When to use V1 vs V2

- **V1 (original camera-focus scripts):** when the user just needs a wait time answered. The link is the answer. Conversion is bonus.
- **V2 (alert-focus scripts):** when posting an ORIGINAL post or replying to a vent/complaint. You're not just answering the question — you're inviting them to never have the question again. Higher conversion ceiling but reads slightly more "I have a thing to share." Use ~40% V1, 60% V2.

---

## When to retire / replace these

If conversion drops or a group flags Nati's posts → rotate to fresh variants. The 12 above are the starting batch. Add 5-10 more after first week based on which ones got the best engagement.

**How to measure:** check Vercel analytics → `/camaras` and `/cruzar/<slug>` page views the day after posting. Spike = post worked. Flat = post got buried or shadowbanned.
